# Chart Selection Justification

This document explains the rationale behind each visualization used in the Tableau Executive Dashboard.

---

## 1. Sales Trend View

**Chart Type:** Line Chart

### Reason

A line chart is the most appropriate visualization for showing sales over time.

It enables stakeholders to:

- Identify trends
- Detect seasonality
- Observe monthly fluctuations
- Monitor business growth

---

## 2. Regional Performance View

**Chart Type:** Horizontal Bar Chart

### Reason

A horizontal bar chart makes it easy to compare sales performance across multiple states.

Color encoding represents profit, allowing users to compare both revenue and profitability simultaneously.

---

## 3. Category Profitability View

**Chart Type:** Horizontal Bar Chart

### Reason

The chart compares profit across categories and sub-categories.

This allows decision makers to quickly identify:

- High-performing product groups
- Low-performing product groups

---

## 4. Customer Segment View

**Chart Type:** Horizontal Bar Chart

### Reason

Customer segments are categorical variables.

Bar charts provide an effective comparison of sales across different customer segments while displaying profit information in tooltips.

---

## 5. Shipping Performance View

**Chart Type:** Stacked Bar Chart

### Reason

A stacked bar chart shows:

- Sales by shipping mode
- Distribution of delivery delay buckets

This visualization highlights how delivery performance differs across shipping methods.

---

## 6. Discount vs Profit View

**Chart Type:** Scatter Plot

### Reason

Scatter plots are ideal for examining relationships between two numerical variables.

This visualization demonstrates how discount percentage impacts profitability while allowing segmentation by customer segment.

---

## 7. Return Analysis View

**Chart Type:** Horizontal Bar Chart

### Reason

The chart compares returned orders across categories while displaying Return Rate.

It enables stakeholders to identify categories requiring quality or process improvements.

---

## Dashboard Design Principles

The dashboard follows standard executive reporting practices:

- Minimal visual clutter
- Clear KPI cards
- Consistent color usage
- Interactive filters
- Business-focused visualizations
- Appropriate chart selection based on analytical objective
